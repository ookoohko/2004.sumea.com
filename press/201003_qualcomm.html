LAS VEGAS 20-10-2003 PRESS RELEASE

LEADING EUROPEAN PUBLISHER SUMEA EXPANDS AVAILABILITY OF AWARD-WINNING
WIRELESS GAMES TO OPERATORS OFFERING BREW™ SERVICES WORLDWIDE
Finnish Mobile Entertainment Powerhouse Announces Roster of BREW-based Games

Sumea, an award-winning game publisher, today announced three of its most popular, premium wireless game titles, developed for QUALCOMM’s Binary Runtime Environment for Wireless™ (BREW) platform, are available for operators that have deployed BREW-based products and services. Users will soon be able to play and enjoy Yo-Yo Fighter, Fantasy Warrior and Racing Fever 2 on their BREW-enabled handsets.

The three popular titles include:

Yo-Yo Fighter — A platform-style game in which the player controls the hero Yozo in his attempt to rescue his friend Jaco who is lost on the World of Stone. The game features a total of 17 action-packed levels with plenty of obstacles to negotiate and lots of baddies to slay... with a yo-yo! Superb graphics, a great storyline and the odd cheat or two, make the game play of this new title totally addictive and captivating for a wide audience.

Fantasy Warrior — A story-driven, role-play adventure game with multiple characters, monsters, dungeons and collectable items, in which the player controls a boy called Rento, the hero. Rento must find a way to dispel the sealed dungeon’s spell while doing favors for people along the way who can help in his quest. By practicing Rento’s fighting skills with fearless sword masters and collecting uniquely mysterious artifacts, you will finally get to meet the fearsome demons and cyclops who guard the gate to the Mountain of Darkness.

Racing Fever 2 — This sequel to Racing Fever—Sumea’s most famous game to date and the benchmark for mobile racing games—includes multiple tracks, road types, weather conditions and opponent drivers. The objective in Racing Fever 2 is to drive better and faster than your current opponent to unlock the next track. But you’d better make sure that you don’t collide with slower cars on the road, as this will slow you right down!

“We currently offer some of the most popular wireless games available today to users in more than 40 countries worldwide,” said Ilkka Paananen, CEO of Sumea. “By expanding our wireless footprint to include BREW-based content, we are effectively opening the doors to new revenue possibilities around the world. We have been impressed with the BREW system and all of the products and services it provides to assist our team with the development and deployment of our top wireless content.”

“The decision by Sumea, a widely renowned wireless game publisher, to develop its content for the BREW platform speaks volumes about the support we have received from the European development community,” said Mike Yuen, director, BREW Developer Relations, QUALCOMM Internet Services. “We’re glad to have Sumea come on board, as one of our primary goals is to continue to grow and empower our international development community with the distribution of their BREW-based applications to operators around the globe.”

QUALCOMM’s BREW system provides products and services that connect the mobile marketplace value chain, which includes application developers, publishers, content providers, device manufacturers, operators and consumers.

Publishers and developers worldwide are generating revenue from BREW-based applications and content, and 19 manufacturers have offered more than 90 BREW-enabled device models to consumers. BREW is successfully enabling the commercial wireless data services of many very successful operators, including Verizon Wireless, ALLTEL, Midwest Wireless and U.S. Cellular in the United States, China Unicom, KDDI in Japan, KTF in South Korea, Telstra in Australia, VIVO in Brazil and BellSouth International in Colombia.

About Sumea
