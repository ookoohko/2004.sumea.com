HELSINKI, FINLAND 09-02-2003 PRESS RELEASE

The UK just can't get enough of Sumea's Java-games — Now it's T-Mobile's turn to jump aboard!

T-Mobile, the second largest mobile operator in Europe, has added five of Sumea's mobile Java-games to its service offering in the United Kingdom. The games are Zoy's Rescue Mission 1 and 2, Stalk 'n' Shoot, Racing Fever and Racing Fever Deluxe.

Thanks to O2, Orange and Vodafone, Sumea's games have in recent months literally taken over the UK. Now 12,5 million T-Mobile customers have the chance to see what they have been missing out on and turn their mobile phones into cutting edge game decks with modern day mobile game classics like Zoy's Rescue Mission and Racing Fever.

—The name Sumea means entertainment for everyone. With T-Mobile on board, millions of new people have the chance to get their hands on the "real experience" — something we are very proud of, says Ilkka Paananen, CEO of Sumea.

More information and game graphics: Ilkka Paananen, CEO, tel + 358 40 594 8173, ilkka@sumea.com


Sumea
Sumea is an award-winning Finnish game publisher that has specialized in Java-technology since it was founded in 1999. Sumea focuses on creating high quality, innovative and technically superior games for Java-enabled mobile phones. Today, Sumea's Java games are available through several channels all over the world, including Nokia, Vodafone, O2, AT&T (United States), WIND (Italy), CSL (Hong Kong), Sunday (Hong Kong), Singtel (Singapore), Sonera (Finland) and Orange.


T-Mobile
T-Mobile is part of one of the largest mobile companies in the world. It is the UK network of T-Mobile International, one of the four strategic pillars of Deutsche Telekom AG. Deutsche Telekom's subsidiaries and affiliated companies serve over 80 million mobile customers worldwide. In addition to the UK, it operates mobile companies in the USA, Germany, Austria, the Netherlands, Czech Republic, Poland, and Russia.
